1. Description of Service

Fud AI is a free, open-source nutrition tracking application available on iOS and Android. It uses AI to estimate the nutritional content of food from photos, iOS Share Extension images, two-photo Camera + Camera captures, photo-library images, optional meal notes, nutrition labels, text descriptions, voice input, and manual entry. It can also look up packaged foods by barcode using Open Food Facts when public product nutrition is available. It also includes smart serving units such as slices, pieces, cups, or ml when the AI can infer them, optional food log sorting from the Home screen, customizable Home nutrient cards, decimal nutrient totals, activity-based protein targets that can display body-weight or lean-mass equivalents when body fat is set, expanded optional nutrient goals for non-macro nutrients, Experimental Energy Burn Goals on iOS and Android, Experimental Adaptive Goals for small weekly weight-trend calorie corrections, weight + optional body-fat % tracking with goal lines, average summaries, net change, and history charts on the Progress tab, an AI Coach chat with on-demand tool calling for personalized weight and nutrition guidance (Coach can fetch any date range of your weight, body-fat, calorie, or food history when you ask, and on Android can include camera/photo attachments), Home Screen and Lock Screen widgets (iOS) / Glance widgets (Android), Apple Watch app and complications on iOS, theme color personalization with matching app / launcher icons, app-update checks from About, bidirectional sync with Apple Health (iOS) and Health Connect (Android), and localization in 15 languages auto-selected by your phone's system language. The app uses Bring Your Own Key access for 13 AI providers, including Gemini 3.5 Flash, and 6 speech-to-text options.

2. AI-Powered Estimates

Nutritional information, optional nutrient-goal suggestions, energy-burn goals, adaptive calorie corrections, weight forecasts, and Coach responses provided by Fud AI are estimated, not exact. AI-powered estimates are generated by your chosen AI provider; Adaptive Goals use local math from your food log, weight trend, and profile. These estimates should be used as general guidance only. Fud AI is not a medical device and does not provide medical advice. Always consult a healthcare professional for dietary or weight-related decisions. The daily calorie target, BMR, TDEE, and macro split shown in the app are computed from peer-reviewed equations (Mifflin-St Jeor or Katch-McArdle for BMR, FAO/WHO/UNU activity multipliers for TDEE, Hall 2011 energy-balance math for goal targets, and activity-based protein targets shown per kg body weight or as an equivalent lean-mass multiplier when body fat is set). Optional detailed goals such as fiber, sugar, fats, cholesterol, sodium, potassium, calcium, iron, magnesium, zinc, vitamins, folate, and omega-3 are separate user settings and do not alter the calorie/protein/carbs/fat calculator. Full citations and source links are available in-app under Settings → Goals & Nutrition → Calculation Methods. Predictive equations carry inherent error (typically ±10% for BMR) and should not replace personalized clinical assessment.

3. AI Coach Chat Disclaimer

The AI Coach tab is a conversational assistant that reads your profile, weight log, food log, current date/timezone, and relevant meal details to produce suggestions. Its replies are generated by the third-party AI provider you select and may be inaccurate, biased, or outdated. Coach is not a substitute for advice from a dietitian, doctor, or other qualified professional. Do not rely on Coach for decisions about medical conditions, eating disorders, pregnancy, or any other health matter that warrants professional guidance.

4. AI Access and API Keys

  • You are responsible for obtaining and managing your own API keys from your chosen AI and speech-to-text providers.
  • API keys are stored locally on your device and are never transmitted to us — in iOS Keychain on iOS, in EncryptedSharedPreferences using AES-256 (Android Keystore-backed) on Android.
  • Usage, rate limits, and billing for AI/STT requests are between you and the provider you chose.

Any usage costs for third-party AI or speech-to-text providers are handled directly by the provider account you configure.

6. Third-Party Providers

When you use the app with an AI or STT provider, your requests (including food photos captured in-app, food photos shared through the iOS Share Extension, meal notes, voice audio, speech-language hints, optional nutrient-goal estimation prompts, Coach messages, and any images attached to Coach) are sent directly from your device to that provider. When you scan a barcode, the barcode value is sent to Open Food Facts for product lookup. Each provider has its own terms and privacy policy that govern how they handle that data. You are responsible for reviewing and accepting those providers' terms. We do not control, endorse, or make representations about third-party providers or public product databases.

Auto-routed providers (OpenRouter openrouter/free): The new free-tier OpenRouter default model auto-selects the upstream provider that handles your request — currently rotating among free vision endpoints from Baidu, Nvidia, and Google, with the available pool changing over time as OpenRouter updates its free tier. By selecting openrouter/free you accept that the upstream provider chosen by OpenRouter at request time is outside our control and may change without notice. If you require a fixed upstream, pick a specific provider model (e.g. google/gemini-2.5-flash, openai/gpt-4o) instead of the auto-routed default.

Barcode lookup: Barcode results depend on Open Food Facts coverage and data quality. If a product is missing or has incomplete nutrition, Fud AI may not be able to create an entry from the barcode and may ask you to use Nutrition Label scan instead.

Nutritional estimates, BMR/TDEE values, macro targets, and weight forecasts are approximations based on published formulas (Katch-McArdle, Mifflin-St Jeor) and energy-balance math (7700 kcal ≈ 1 kg); they may not be accurate for all individuals. 9. Apple Health & Health Connect Integration

By enabling Apple Health (iOS) or Health Connect (Android) integration, you authorize the app to read and write health data as described in our Privacy Policy. Written data includes body measurements (weight, height, body fat percentage where supported) and per-meal nutrition. The app can also read weight + body-fat samples back into your profile + Progress chart, so external readings from compatible apps or smart scales you've connected (Apple Watch, Withings, Renpho, Samsung Health, Fitbit, etc.) can flow in automatically. If you enable Energy Burn Goals in Goal Settings, the app may also read active and total energy burned from Apple Health or Health Connect to estimate calorie targets while keeping protein, carbs, and fat editable. Where supported, historical backfill imports past weight + body-fat samples so the chart starts populated. On iOS, you can revoke access via Settings → Health → Data Access & Devices → Fud AI; on Android, via the Health Connect app. Note that Delete All Data in the app only wipes local storage — your Apple Health / Health Connect samples persist unless you remove them separately.

11. Availability

The app requires an internet connection for AI food analysis, barcode lookup, Coach chat, remote speech-to-text, and app-update checks. Fully offline operation is possible only when you use Ollama (local LLM) and the on-device speech recognizer (Native iOS or Native Android). Offline functionality otherwise is limited to viewing previously logged data, running local BMR/TDEE math, manual entry, reading from existing Apple Health / Health Connect samples, and re-logging from Saved Meals. Some Android OEM skins (OriginOS, MIUI/HyperOS, One UI) aggressively kill background alarms; the app exposes a Battery Optimization deep-link in Settings to whitelist Fud AI so scheduled reminders fire reliably — without whitelisting, reminders may be silently delayed or dropped.
